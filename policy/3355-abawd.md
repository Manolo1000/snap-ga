# Section 3355 — Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWD)

Source: Georgia DFCS SNAP Policy Manual (MT-87, June 2026)
URL: https://pamms.dhs.ga.gov/dfcs/snap/3355/

---

ABAWDs are subject to a time-limited benefit rule and a work requirement.

## Who is an ABAWD

An individual is an ABAWD if ALL of the following are true:
- Age 18 through 52 (raised from 49 by the Fiscal Responsibility Act of 2023, phased in through FY 2024)
- Not physically or mentally unfit for employment
- Not caring for a child under age 18 in the household
- Not pregnant
- Not otherwise exempt (see below)

## Time limit

ABAWDs may receive SNAP for no more than 3 months in a 36-month period unless they meet the work requirement or are exempt. This is often called "3-in-36."

## Work requirement

To keep receiving SNAP beyond the 3-month limit, an ABAWD must, each month:
- Work at least 80 hours (roughly 20 hours per week), OR
- Participate in an approved work program (including SNAP Employment and Training / SNAP Works) for at least 80 hours, OR
- Do a combination totaling at least 80 hours, OR
- Participate in workfare

## Exemptions

An ABAWD is exempt from the time limit and work requirement if they are:
- Under 18 or 52 or older
- Physically or mentally unfit for employment (verifiable by evidence)
- Pregnant
- Responsible for a dependent child under 18 in the household (regardless of whether the child is in the AU)
- A veteran (any discharge status other than dishonorable)
- Experiencing homelessness
- Age 24 or younger and formerly in foster care on their 18th birthday (or a higher age up to 21 if the state extended foster care)
- Receiving unemployment compensation or applied for it
- Enrolled at least half-time in school or a training program
- Regularly participating in a drug or alcohol treatment program
- Living in an area with an approved ABAWD waiver (some Georgia counties, subject to federal approval — check current status)

## Regaining eligibility after loss

An ABAWD who loses SNAP by exhausting the 3-month limit can regain eligibility by working 80 hours in a 30-day period, or by becoming exempt.

## Notes for the eligibility tool

- ABAWD status affects only that individual's eligibility, not the whole household. Other household members remain eligible under general rules.
- The 3-month clock is a running 36-month period, not a calendar year.
- Georgia's waived counties change over time based on unemployment. If the tool cannot verify current waiver status for the user's county, it should note this and refer the user to DFCS.
