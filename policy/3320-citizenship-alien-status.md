# Section 3320

Source: Georgia DFCS SNAP Policy Manual (MT-87, June 2026)
URL: https://pamms.dhs.ga.gov/dfcs/snap/3320/
Extracted from PDF pages 156-176

---

SNAP eligibility for non-citizens is governed by federal immigration status categories.

Eligible immigrant categories include (non-exhaustive):
- Lawful Permanent Residents (LPRs / green card holders), with a 5-year waiting period unless exempt
- Refugees (Section 207 INA), asylees, Cuban/Haitian entrants, Iraqi/Afghan Special Immigrants — no waiting period
- Amerasian immigrants, victims of trafficking (T visa), COFA citizens (Micronesia, Marshall Islands, Palau)
- Non-citizen U.S. nationals (American Samoa, Swains Island) — treated as citizens for SNAP

Exemptions from the 5-year waiting period for LPRs include:
- Children under 18
- Elderly aliens (born on or before 8/22/1931 and lawfully residing in U.S. on 8/22/1996)
- Blind or disabled aliens receiving disability benefits
- Aliens with a military connection (active duty, honorably discharged veteran, or their spouse/children)
- LPRs with 40 qualifying quarters of work history

Ineligible categories include most non-immigrants (tourists, students on F-1, most H visa holders),
undocumented individuals, and certain parolees.

Mixed-status households: U.S. citizen children and eligible immigrant members of a household with
ineligible non-citizen members may still qualify for SNAP. The eligible members' benefits are
calculated on a pro-rata basis. Ineligible members do not count toward household size but a portion
of their income may still be counted (deemed).

Sponsor deeming: For LPRs whose entry was sponsored, the sponsor's income and resources may be
deemed to the immigrant for eligibility purposes, subject to exceptions (indigence, abuse, sponsor
death).

CRITICAL: Immigration status determinations are complex and consequential. This tool does not
adjudicate any immigration-related eligibility question. Any user situation involving non-citizen
household members must be referred to legal aid (Georgia Legal Services 1-833-457-7529, or a local
immigration legal aid provider). Cite this section (3320) but do NOT issue a "likely_eligible" or
"likely_ineligible" verdict for households with any non-citizen member.
